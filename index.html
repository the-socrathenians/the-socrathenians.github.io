Abstract

In a university split across multiple campuses, especially if those campuses are in different cities across different countries, connection between people is harder than need be. This holds even more true for cases wherein one campus much smaller and newer than the other, both within the new as well as across either campus. The commute is no less difficult, a part of daily life dreaded by many and frequently neither productive nor relaxing.

We aim to address two problems: the difficulty in intra- and interconnectivity as it pertains to ECH, as well as making the commute between ETH in Zurich and ECH both pleasant and even fun. So far no ideal solution exists that addresses either problem. Our product is an elegant solution to both problems at once, with a potentially much broader application.

The key feature we chose to implement for the live demo is the matching algorithm, which matches people across overall similarity on the bulk of topics which interest them. The reasoning for this specific approach was that merely limiting the matching to one or two ideal topic matches will also gravely limit the possible conversation topics as well as how heuristically likely it is for any two people to get along - this way, we maximize the probability of a conversation not only taking place, but being a pleasant and profitable experience and opportunity for social learning.

This app has an application both within the commute, aiming to connect (with best effort) two or more people for a lively discussion, be it for expanding research possibilities, exchanging valuable knowledge and skills across students, or simply a friendly conversation to strengthen the bonds within and across the campuses. The app's design however is very general purpose, allowing for it to be used in a wider variety of situations, such as during breaks in a new lecture or a dining hall which, despite being full, feels hard to connect with others.
